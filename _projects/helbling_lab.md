## Helbling Robotics Research Lab

The Helbling Robotics Research Lab investigates the development of autonomous insect-scale robotic systems designed to operate reliably in complex, real-world environments. I joined the lab this semester as part of my graduate research, where I am contributing to the development of one of the group’s primary robotic platforms, Gammabot, by designing and implementing a dynamic braking mechanism. Gammabot is a flapping-wing robot that travels along the air–water interface, using small-scale fluid–structure interactions to achieve controlled interfacial locomotion and maneuverability.
