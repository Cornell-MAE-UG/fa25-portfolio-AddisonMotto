## Interactive Wind Turbine Exhibit

During my internship at Creative Engineering LLC last summer, I was given an intern design project to create an interactive wind turbine for an educational museum exhibit. Working alongside another intern, I was responsible for the full design, fabrication, and installation of the system under a strict deadline. The project was successfully completed and installed in time for the exhibit opening, providing a hands-on learning experience for museum visitors.

## System Description

The exhibit features an interactive hand-driven mechanism that allows museum visitors to directly power the wind turbine. A large external spinning wheel is connected to a concealed drivetrain inside the nacelle, where rotational input is transmitted through a stepper motor and custom gearbox to actuate the turbine blades. This design creates a clear cause-and-effect learning experience, demonstrating how mechanical input can be converted into rotational motion similar to real wind energy systems.

Key engineering challenges included designing a structurally robust tower and nacelle capable of withstanding continuous public use, integrating and concealing all electronics and wiring within the structure, and developing a reliable gearbox with appropriate tolerances for smooth operation. Additional safety considerations led to the implementation of a clutch mechanism that limits transmitted torque to prevent injury from rotating blades. Significant attention was also given to aesthetic design, surface finishing, and overall visual appeal to ensure the exhibit was both engaging and durable in a high-traffic educational environment.
